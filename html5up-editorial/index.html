About me

I am a PhD student in the Department of Computer Science at Boise State University, Idaho. I work in the NASA SnowEx research group under the supervision of Dr. Hans-Peter Marshall and Professor Jodi Mead. The research group develops and applies machine learning techniques to remote sensing of the cryosphere. My research interest intersects Machine Learning, Deep Learning application in Remote Sensing, and Simulation.

I obtained my MS degree in Computer Science from Texas A&M University-Commerce where I developed simulation models for optimizing biofuel production using AnyLogic, OptQuest and Java. I am an AI enthusiast, passionate about applying machine learning methods to different fields for process optimization and transforming data into compact and actionable information to improve organizational productivity and efficiency.

I am passionate about understanding the underlying mathematical statistical methods behind data analysis and machine learning to ensure best use case for different scenarios.
