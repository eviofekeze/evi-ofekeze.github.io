Research

User-centered AI Explanations

I work under the supervision of Dr. Ronald Metoyer (Associate Professor of Computer Science; Head, Human Computer Interaction Lab at University of Notre Dame). This research project entails designing and evaluating user-centered AI explanations. Our current focus is in the software traceability domain. This work is supported by the NSF Grant with award number 1901059.

AI-powered Social Service Access

I work with Dr. Ronald Metoyer and Professor Ann-Marie Conrado on a research project that is focused on facilitating access to social services, especially for vulnerable populations. This interdisciplinary research project involves designing a user-centered interface, as well as a robust recommendation algorithm to provide users with personalized social services that align with their information need. This work is supported by the Jesse Ball duPont Fund.

Child Adaptive Search Tool

I worked along side a team of researchers in a National Science Foundation funded grant to design, develop, and research child-adaptive search interfaces and child-oriented information retrieval algorithms. This work was supported by the NSF Grant with award number 1763649.

Information Retrieval for Children

For over 3 years, I worked on research projects that involved designing interfaces and implemented information retrieval algorithms centered on enhancing children's experiences when utilizing search engines. This work involved designing a child-oriented query suggestion algorithm, a multi-objective algorithm for ranking search results, as well as conducting a number of exploratory analysis to understand search engines limitations in handling children's searches and children's perception about this web technology. This work was supported by the NSF Grant with award number 1565937.

Bias in Recommender Systems

I participated in a research project where we performed offline evaluation and examined a utility-based metric of recommendation effectiveness to explore whether different user demographic groups experience similar recommendation accuracy.

ToastBot: AI-powered compliments generator

For my deep learning class, I worked on a research project where we built a deep learning model that can automatically generate compliments for users, given their picture and a short description of their mood. The aim was to help users feeling sad or depressed feel better by receiving automatically generated personalized compliments.
